Research

Haptics

In daily life, we are always receiving and processing tactile information: When grasping an object, touching a fabric, pushing a door. Tactile information is fundamental to properly interact with the environment and other people, this leads to pursue the development of new methods and tools to both understand human tactile perception and artificially reproduce tactile information. Haptic (from Greek haptesthai, related to touch), now understood as the science of human touch, studies the tactile human sensing and manipulation. The capability of accurately represent textures allows us to get more realistic sensations when interacting with a virtual (or remote) texture. Most of the currently used methods to render haptic textures are based on acceleration measurements that sometimes could be inaccurate due to the dependency on velocity and applied force when recording. Im working to improve haptic texture modeling in tool-mediated texture scenario.

Animation

Phase change phenomena is present in our daily life in situations such as the evaporation of a fluid when it reaches its boiling temperature or condensation of water present in air due pressure changes or even difference of temperature in boundaries or melting of snow when winter is ending. I'm working in a method to simulate phase transition phenomena based on latent heat of materials using Position-based dynamics and smoothed particle hydrodinamics.

Challenges

Ultrahaptics

We (Steeven Villa and Victor Adriel ) are one of the 5 teams selected to show a demo at the EuroHaptics 2018 . We are the Bazil team, presenting UltraMotion, a robotic way to extend mid-air haptics to largest work volumes.

3DUI

In the context of the 3DUI Contest promoted by the IEEE VR 2018, we propose 3D interaction techniques that address three distinct tasks in a virtual environment setup: climbing a ladder, controlling a quadcopter in a first-person view, and building a tower by stacking a series of objects. The interaction techniques were developed so the player, our 3D-athlete, has control over the events in each task, following metaphors that facilitate the use of the interface, and having status and spatial awareness supported by clear feedback cues. Thus, the player should be able to execute the tasks with precision and agility.
